STEVE JOBS

  • 1987

    Jefferson Award for Public Service

  • 1989

    Entrepreneur of the Decade by Inc

  • 1991

    Howard Vollum Award from Reed College

  • 2004 - 2010

    Listed among the Time 100 Most Influential People in the World on five separate occasions

  • 2007

    Named the most powerful person in business by Fortune magazine

    Inducted into the California Hall of Fame, located at The California Museum for History, Women and the Arts

  • 2012

    Grammy Trustees Award, an award for those who have influenced the music industry in areas unrelated to performance

    Posthumously inducted as a Disney Legend

  • 2017

    Steve Jobs Theatre opens at Apple Park

  • 2022

    Posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by US President Joe Biden, the country's highest civilian honor

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur and inventor who co-founded Apple Inc. and transformed the technology industry with his innovative products and leadership. Known for his intense and perfectionistic leadership style, Jobs led Apple to introduce the Macintosh computer in 1984 and later transformed the company with innovative products such as the iPod, iPhone, and iPad.


In addition to his work at Apple, Jobs was involved in other ventures, such as Pixar Animation Studios, which he helped transform into a major animation studio. Pixar's films, including Toy Story and Finding Nemo, were critically acclaimed and commercially successful, establishing Jobs as a visionary leader in the entertainment industry.


Jobs was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2003 and underwent a liver transplant in 2009. He stepped down as CEO of Apple in 2011, citing health reasons, and passed away later that year at the age of 56. Steve Jobs' legacy continues to inspire entrepreneurs and innovators worldwide, and he will be remembered as one of the most influential figures of his time.
